Fever in Children: What Parents Should Know

Dr. M. Quamrul Hassan

Fever is one of the most common reasons parents worry and seek medical care. While fever itself is not a disease, it is an important sign that the body is responding to an infection or inflammation.

What is Fever?

Fever is defined as a body temperature of 38°C (100.4°F) or higher. It is part of the immune system’s natural defense mechanism.

Important: Fever alone does not indicate severity. A child’s behavior and overall condition are often more important than the number.

Common Causes of Fever

Most fevers in children are caused by viral infections such as common cold, influenza, or viral gastroenteritis. Bacterial infections are less common but may require antibiotics.

When Should Parents Worry?

Seek medical attention immediately if:
  • Baby is less than 3 months old
  • Child is unusually drowsy or unresponsive
  • Breathing is difficult
  • Persistent vomiting or seizures occur

How to Manage Fever at Home

Ensure adequate fluids, keep the child comfortable, and use antipyretics such as paracetamol when necessary. Avoid unnecessary medications.
